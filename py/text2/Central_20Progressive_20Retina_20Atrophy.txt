During the years 2000 to 2004, the writer treated CPRA patients with xiao yao san with added ingredients and had satisfactory results. 1. Clinical data 1.1. General data Total outpatients: 71 cases (84 eyes) Time: December, 2000 to December, 2004, Sexes: male 40 patients, female 3l patients Sickness in eyes: Single eye morbidity 58 cases, both eyes morbidity 13 cases; Courses of illnesses: 5~57days, average 31days; age 20~52 years old. We divided the patients into 2 groups, each with 42 morbid eyes: (a) complementary of Chinese and Western medicine treatment group (b) Control group of western medicine treatment group 1. 2. Criteria of diagnosis 2. Treatment method 2.1 Chinese herb therapy: Prescription: xiao yao san with added ingredients. chai hu 12 g ﹐ dang gui wei (tail) 12 g ﹐ bai shao 10 g ﹐ bai zhu (炒) 10 g ﹐ sheng jiang 50 克﹐ fu ling 10 g ﹐ gan cao 3 g ﹐ bo he 8 g ﹐ shan yao 10g , che qian zi 10g , chong wei zi 10g , dan shen 10g , chuang xiong 10g , gou qi zi 15g. Boil with water. One dose per day. 10 days is a therapy period. For every therapy period, take a 3 day break. After 1 to 4 therapy period, observe effectiveness of the therapy. 3.
