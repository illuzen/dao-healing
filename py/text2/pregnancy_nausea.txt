Edited by: Joe Hing Kwok Chu 按此看中文 Nausea is common during early stages of pregnancy. It usually lasts for about three months but some women may still have them into fourth or fifth month of pregnancy. Pregnant women without morning sickness have more than double the incidences of miscarriage than those with morning sickness. Some studies show that 10% of those without morning sickness had miscarriage while those with morning sickness had 4% of miscarriage. One of the formulae can be used: Formula 1: Taking 1 to 2 grams of pickled ginger (ginger soaked in vinegar), 3 times per day may help relieve nausea. Pickled ginger is available in Chinese or Japanese grocery stores or make it yourself at home. Formula 2: dang shen 9 g, dang gui 9 g, bai zhu 9 g, bai shao 12 g, fu ling 12 g, chen pi 6 g, liu shen qu 12 g, sha ren 3 g (to be added to the pot during last 5 minutes of cooking), jiang ban xia 9 g. Among them 4.5% healed in 6 days.
