It may come with the feeling of creepiness in the skin and involuntary movement of the legs. Causes of restless leg syndrome can be: 1. A chronic disease , such as: kidney failure , diabetes, peripheral neuropathy. Therapy improves symptoms often improve over restless leg symptoms. 2, Certain drugs may aggravate restless leg symptoms, such as: Dopamine receptor blocking drugs: The anti-nausea drugs : prochlorperazine or metoclopramide hydrochloride (metoclopramide; trade name : metoclopramide, Reglan, Reglan ODT, Metozol ODT, Octamide, Maxolon ( this name has been discontinued ) metoclopramide (Maxolon) , also known as the monohydrochloride salt, metoclopramide (Paspertin), dihydrochloride Primperan). Antipsychotics , such as: haloperidol (Haloperidol) or phenothiazine derivatives. antidepressants: increase serotonin drugs , Celexa (citalopram) Fluvoxamine maleate, Luvox (fluvoxamine) Prozac (fluoxetine HCl) Paxil (paroxetine) Zoloft (sertraline) cold and allergy medicines containing sedating antihistamines : such as Allegra, Benadryl, Claritin, Chlor-Trimeton, Dimetane, Zyrtec, Tavist etc 3 . Pregnancy, especially in the last three months. After four weeks of production , general symptoms usually disappear. 4 dysfunction circuits by using amine neurotransmitters dopa God basal ganglia [ 1 ] 5 Lack of vitamin B12 6. Traumatic brain injury Below are commonly used drugs for restless leg syndrome (RLS) and periodic limb movement disorder ( PLMD ) Drugs being used for RLS and PLMD Dopamine agonists: L-dopa, carbidopa (Sinemet) pergolide mesylate (Permax) pramipexole (Mirapex) ropinirole (Requip) Benzodiazepines clonazepam (Klonopin) temazepam(restoril) diazepam (valium) etc Anti-convulsants gabapentin (Neurontin) carbamazepine (Tegretol) Opioids (narcotics): codeine oxycodone propoxyphene (Darvon) methadone, etc Note: Dopamine agonists stimulate the receptors in nerves in the brain that normally would be stimulated by dopamine. Unlike levodopa, a dopamine agonist is not changed (converted) into dopamine when it enters the body, but it behaves like dopamine. Benzodiazepine works by decreasing abnormal electrical activity in the brain. Carbamazepine is used alone or in combination with other medications to control certain types of seizures. Pergolide msylate (Permax) has been withdrawn from the U.S. market since March 29, 2007 due to the possibility of heart valve damage. It is used to treat Parkinson's disease, hyperprolactinemia, stiffness, tremors, spasms, and poor muscle control, and restless leg syndrome.
