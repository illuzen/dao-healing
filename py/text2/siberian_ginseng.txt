"Siberian ginseng" products that are sold in America have nothing in similar with any kind of ginseng . The Siberian genseng plant is a shrub of 1 to 3 meters tall, while the ginseng plant is herbaceous. It does not have any similarity in looks to Chinese ginseng, Korean ginseng or American ginseng. It is called Siberian ginseng in America for advertising purposes. It was first introduced into the American herb market in the late 1970s as "Wuchaseng" and "Wujiaseng." (wu jia shen 五加蔘) There appears to be no historical precedent in Chinese traditions to applying the qualifier "seng ( 蔘 or short for ginseng )" to E. senticosus (Shiu Ying Hu, 1979). The correct Chinese name is ci wu jia. The United States Congressional amendment to the Federal Food, Drug and Cosmetic Act  signed into law in May 2002  eliminates any confusion regarding what is true ginseng. Now only the genus Panax can be called ginseng on labeling or in advertising.
